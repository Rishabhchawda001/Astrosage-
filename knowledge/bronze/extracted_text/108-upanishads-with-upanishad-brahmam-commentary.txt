PREFACE

Tae present volume contains the first eight of the
ten Major Upanisads, namely, Isa, Kena, Katha, Prasna,
Mundaka, Mandikya, Taittiriya and Aitareya, along
with the commentary of Upanisadbrahmayogin. In this
series have already appeared the ninety-eight Minor
Upanigads with the same commentary; there has also
appeared another volume containing seventy-one more
Upanigads, most of which have not till now been published.
The commentary of the Upanisadbrahmayogin is the
only commentary available for all the Minor Upanisads.
For the ten Major Upanisads there are other commen-
taries. The present commentary follows more or less
the Bhisya of Sankaracarya. Yet this is not a mere
repetition of Saikara’s Bhisya, In many places this
sommentary is more elaborate and as such it is sure
‘o be useful for those who want to understand the
Advaita interpretation of the Upanisads. The text
‘ollowed in this edition is the usual text found in other
ditions. But in the case of the Aitareyopanisad,
Sahkaracarya comments on the text beginning with
‘atma va idam eka evigra asit”. This is the fourth
hapter in the second Aranyaka of the Aitareyaranyaka

=== PAGE 5 ===

and Sankaracarya comments on the fourth, fifth, sixth and
seventh chapters of the second Aranyaka. Upanigad-
brahmayogin comments from the first chapter of the
second Aranyaka beginning with “ega pantha etat
karmaitat satyam”. Since the present commentary
follows Sankara, it lends itself to a conjecture that
Sankara too had commented the text from the first
chapter.

The remaining two Upanisads, Chandogya and
Brhadaranyaka will appear as the next volume in the

series.
